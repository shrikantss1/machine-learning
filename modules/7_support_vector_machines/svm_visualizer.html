Support Vector Machines (SVM)

Explore the "Widest Street" margin and the dimensional magic of the Kernel Trick.

1. The "Widest Street" (Margin)

Notice how the solid Decision Boundary is placed perfectly in the middle. The dashed lines are the "sidewalks" (Margins). The points with the glowing halos are the Support Vectors—they are the only points the algorithm cares about!

2. The Kernel Trick (Concentric Circles)

On the left, the data is trapped in 2D. You cannot draw a straight line to separate the red and blue dots.
On the right, the SVM uses a Kernel to calculate a 3rd dimension (Z = X² + Y²). Suddenly, the data forms a bowl, and the SVM can easily slide a flat sheet of glass (the Hyperplane) right between them!
